What is ADHD?

ADHD affects attention, impulse control, and energy levels â€” and also comes with creativity and unique strengths.

Common Signs & Symptoms

  • Difficulty focusing or staying on task
  • Forgetfulness and disorganization
  • Impulsivity or restlessness
  • Hyperfocus on interesting activities

ADHD Strengths

  • Creativity and out-of-the-box thinking
  • High energy and enthusiasm
  • Strong passion for interests
  • Problem-solving abilities

How to Help People with ADHD

  • Break tasks into smaller steps
  • Use reminders and visual cues
  • Encourage movement breaks
  • Be patient and supportive

Why ADHD Awareness Matters in Society

ADHD awareness helps reduce stigma and misunderstanding. Many people with ADHD are often labeled as lazy, careless, or disruptive, when in reality their brains simply process information differently. Understanding ADHD allows schools, workplaces, and communities to provide fair support and equal opportunities.

When society is informed about ADHD, people are more likely to receive early diagnosis, helpful accommodations, and acceptance. Awareness also highlights the strengths people with ADHD bring â€” such as creativity, energy, and innovative thinking â€” which benefit society as a whole.
