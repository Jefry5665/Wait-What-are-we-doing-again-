What is ADHD?

ADHD affects attention, impulse control, and energy levels. It also comes with creativity and unique strengths.

Common Signs & Symptoms

  • Difficulty focusing
  • Forgetfulness
  • Impulsivity
  • Restlessness or hyperfocus

ADHD Strengths

  • Creativity
  • High energy
  • Problem-solving skills

How to Help People with ADHD

  • Break tasks into smaller, manageable steps
  • Use reminders, planners, or visual schedules
  • Encourage movement and short breaks
  • Be patient, supportive, and understanding

Why ADHD Awareness Matters in Society

ADHD awareness helps reduce stigma and misunderstanding. Many people with ADHD are often labeled as lazy, careless, or disruptive, when in reality their brains simply process information differently. Understanding ADHD allows schools, workplaces, and communities to provide fair support and equal opportunities.

When society is informed about ADHD, people are more likely to receive early diagnosis, helpful accommodations, and acceptance. Awareness also highlights the strengths people with ADHD bring — such as creativity, energy, and innovative thinking — which benefit society as a whole.
